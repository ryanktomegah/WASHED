# Founder Validation Memo

**Date:** 2026-04-30
**Status:** Founder-confirmed qualitative validation for continued build and internal beta preparation.

## Decision

The founder confirms direct conversations with washerwomen and potential customers in the target market. The feedback was that Washed solves a high-value problem: unreliable laundry help for households and unstable income for washerwomen.

This memo replaces the requirement for formal pre-build field interviews as a blocker to continued product development. It does not replace post-build usability testing, provider verification, payment readiness, or real operational onboarding.

## Evidence Summary

Founder-provided signal:

- Washerwomen understand the value of steadier work and platform-mediated assignments.
- Potential customers understand the value of scheduled laundry help, predictable pricing, and support if something goes wrong.
- The service model fits an existing behavior pattern rather than forcing a completely new habit.

## Product Implications

| Area | Decision |
|---|---|
| Continue engineering | Approved |
| Keep smartphone-first flow | Approved for target customers |
| Keep T1/T2 subscription pricing for testing | Approved until real payment data says otherwise |
| Keep worker floor + visit bonus model | Approved for simulation and worker discussion |
| Require formal interview report before coding | Waived by founder |
| Require usability testing before public launch | Still required |

## Confidence Level

Confidence is enough to continue building and to prepare internal testing. It is not yet enough to claim market proof, demand conversion, payment reliability, or worker retention.

## Follow-Up Evidence

Replace assumptions with operating data during closed beta:

- Signup completion rate.
- Payment success rate.
- Assignment time.
- Visit completion rate.
- Worker satisfaction.
- Subscriber satisfaction/NPS.
- Support/dispute volume.
- Worker earnings versus expectations.
