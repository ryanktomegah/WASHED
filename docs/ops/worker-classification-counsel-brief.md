# Worker Classification Counsel Brief - Washed

**Date drafted:** 2026-04-29
**Purpose:** Prepare facts and questions for Togolese counsel to assess whether Washed can launch with workers as independent contractors under the proposed compensation and control model, or whether employment registration is required earlier.

## Question for Counsel

Can Washed classify washerwomen as independent contractors at launch while providing a monthly income floor, uniforms, ID badges, route assignments, app-based check-in/out, quality rules, and platform-mediated payments?

## Proposed Worker Model

- Worker role: in-home washerwoman serving subscribed households.
- Launch status: independent contractor.
- Compensation: 40,000 XOF guaranteed monthly floor plus 600 XOF per completed visit.
- Target full month: 48 visits, giving 68,800 XOF.
- Client payment: subscriber pays platform only; no client-to-worker cash.
- Worker payout: platform pays worker by mobile money.
- Future path: consider conversion of top performers to employment after 12 to 18 months.

## Platform Controls Currently Proposed

- Worker must complete identity verification.
- Worker must provide two references.
- Worker must obtain police clearance.
- Worker receives branded uniform and photo ID badge.
- Worker is assigned to subscribers by platform/operator.
- Worker follows scheduled visits and time windows.
- Worker uses app route list.
- Worker check-in/out is GPS validated.
- Worker takes before/after photos.
- Worker reports issues through app/operator.
- Worker can be suspended after serious theft/damage allegations pending investigation.
- Worker earns visit bonus only on completed visits.

## Counsel Questions

1. Under Togolese law, does the proposed model create employee status despite the contractor label?
2. Which control factors matter most: schedule, uniform, app supervision, route assignment, guaranteed floor, or exclusivity?
3. Can a contractor receive a guaranteed monthly minimum without employment classification?
4. Can contractors be required to wear a uniform and ID badge?
5. Can contractors be required to use app check-in/out and photo proof?
6. What written agreement terms reduce misclassification risk?
7. Are CNSS/INAM registrations required at launch under this model?
8. Are taxes, withholding, or declarations required for contractor payouts?
9. What accident insurance is required or recommended for workers entering client homes?
10. What termination/suspension process is legally required after allegations?
11. What liability does the platform have for worker injury in a client home?
12. What liability does the platform have for theft/damage by a worker?
13. Can the platform require no direct cash payment from clients?
14. What records should be kept for each worker?
15. What changes would make contractor classification more defensible?

## Possible Mitigations to Review

- Written contractor agreement in French and local language where needed.
- No exclusivity requirement.
- Worker can accept or reject route assignments within defined limits.
- Worker can set availability windows.
- Platform provides safety support and payment processing without dictating every work method.
- Clear dispute process before suspension becomes termination.
- Accident insurance for all active workers.
- Record of worker invoices or payout statements.
